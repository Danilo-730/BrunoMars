About Him

Peter Gene Hernandez (Honolulu, October 8, 1985), best known by the stage name Bruno Mars is an American singer, songwriter, music producer, dancer and multi-instrumentalist, born and raised in Hawaii.

Starting

Coming from a family with a great musical tradition, Mars began to sing and perform as an amateur artist during his childhood. After graduating from high school, he decided to move to Los Angeles, California, with the goal of investing more and more in his music career.

Begin of Carreer

In Los Angeles, he formed the team of producers The Smeezingtons, alongside Philip Lawrence and Ari Levine, working for Motown Records. After his failure with the Motown Records label, Mars signed with Atlantic Records in 2009.
During his first months as an artist for the label, he co-wrote the arrangements and made appearances on songs such as the rapper's "Nothin 'on You" (2010) BoB, and "Billionaire" (2010), by American singer Travie McCoy.

The Success

In 2014, Mars collaborated with Mark Ronson on Uptown Funk, a single that performed well on many music charts around the world, including the USA (for 14 consecutive weeks), Australia, Canada, New Zealand and the United Kingdom.
Her third studio album, 24K Magic (2016), received seven Grammy Awards and released the hit singles "24K Magic", "That's What I Like", "Versace on the Floor" and "Finesse"..
